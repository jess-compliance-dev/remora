Sunday mornings in the kitchen

Every Sunday, she woke up before everyone else and opened the kitchen window first. The room always smelled like coffee and something sweet warming in the oven. She would hum quietly to herself while laying out cups, as if she already knew exactly how the morning should feel before anyone else entered the room. It was never loud or dramatic. It was just her way of making the house feel safe.
Why this memory matters
This memory captures her quiet care — the way she created warmth through small rituals, not grand gestures. It reflects comfort, routine, and the feeling of being held by an ordinary moment.
